You've separated the knowledge axis from the behavior axis, mapped the four-spoke tradeoff surface, and learned why the scariest failures throw no errors. This gate checks that you can put it together under production pressure.

<KeyInsightCallout>
Production LLM engineering is a meta-skill in two halves: match the tool to the problem's axis (RAG/ICL change what it KNOWS, fine-tune/distill change how it BEHAVES), then accept that every lever trades latency/cost/quality/reliability — and the deadliest failures are the silent, confident ones that look exactly like success.
</KeyInsightCallout>
