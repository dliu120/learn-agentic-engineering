You've walked the ladder and the loop. The throughline: a schema-valid output is just a well-typed claim from an unreliable, retry-prone producer of untrusted input — so the real reliability work lives in validation, bounded repair, safe fallbacks, and idempotent side effects.

<KeyInsightCallout>
Structure is not the same as truth — guarantee shape with the right rung, but win reliability in the loop: validate as the gate, repair cheap-first and bounded, fall back to a safe typed default, and make every side effect idempotent because the retry WILL happen.
</KeyInsightCallout>
