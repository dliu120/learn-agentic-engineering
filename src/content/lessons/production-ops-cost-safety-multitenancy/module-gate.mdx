You've now seen the three forces that collide once an LLM goes to production: Money (cost hides in the call graph), Trust (the model can't tell instructions from data), and Isolation (one shared system, many tenants). One principle ties them together.

<KeyInsightCallout>
Never let the probabilistic model be the boundary for cost, authorization, or tenant isolation — those belong to deterministic code at the edges: tags across the whole fan-out, authZ at the tool/retrieval edge with the user's identity, and cache/state keyed by full identity.
</KeyInsightCallout>
